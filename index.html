Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam—Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam—was one of India’s most respected scientists and the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his pivotal role in developing India’s ballistic missile and nuclear weapons programs, he was instrumental in projects like Agni and Prithvi missiles and played a key part in the 1998 Pokhran-II nuclear tests. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a humble family, Dr. Kalam overcame many hardships through determination and a passion for learning, earning a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.
Dr. Kalam received numerous honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian award. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while addressing students—a testament to his lifelong dedication to education and youth empowerment. His legacy endures as a symbol of integrity, scientific excellence, and hope for future generations.

Mahendra Singh Dhoni

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, popularly known as MS Dhoni, is one of the greatest cricketers and captains in the history of Indian cricket. Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, he made his international debut in 2004 and quickly rose to fame for his exceptional skills as a wicketkeeper-batsman and his calm, strategic leadership. Dhoni is the only captain to have won all three major ICC trophies: the 2007 ICC T20 World Cup, the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy. Under his captaincy, India also achieved the number one ranking in Test cricket for the first time in 2009. Known for his powerful hitting, especially his signature "helicopter shot," Dhoni scored over 17,000 international runs, including 16 centuries and more than 100 half-centuries, with an impressive average above 50 in ODIs. As a wicketkeeper, he holds records for the most dismissals and stumpings in international cricket. In the Indian Premier League (IPL), Dhoni has been the cornerstone of the Chennai Super Kings franchise, leading them to five IPL titles and two Champions League T20 victories, while also holding numerous records such as most matches played and most wins as captain.
